## Description

Forces all SMB client connections to use encryption (SMB 3.x AES-128-GCM or AES-256-GCM). Connections to servers that do not support SMB encryption will be refused. Introduced as a granular policy in Windows 11 24H2 ADMX templates.

## Impact

- ✅ Prevents man-in-the-middle interception of SMB traffic
- ✅ Eliminates credential relay attacks on unencrypted shares
- ⚠️ Breaks connectivity to legacy SMB servers (Windows 7, older NAS devices) that do not support SMB 3.x encryption
- ⚠️ May increase CPU overhead on high-throughput file servers (negligible on modern hardware with AES-NI)

## Use Cases

- Enterprise environments requiring data-in-transit protection
- Zero-trust network architectures
- Healthcare / finance compliance (HIPAA, PCI-DSS)
- Environments with untrusted network segments (VLAN isolation)
